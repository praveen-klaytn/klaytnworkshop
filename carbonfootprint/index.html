Carbon Footprint

To calculate your emissions, Please check the below source cards that apply to your organization.

Emissions from things you own or control
Scope 1
Stationary Combustion
Fuel-like oil and gas-burned in buildings or equipment owned or operated by your organization.
Mobile sources
Fuel your organization purchases for owned or leased vehicles and mobile equipment (e.g., cars, trucks, gas-powered tools).
Fugitive emissions - Refrigeration
Chemical releases from AC and refrigeration equipment your organization owns or controls.
Fugitive emissions - Fire suppression
Chemical releases from or use of building fire suppression systems or equipment (like fire extinguishers) your organization owns or controls.
Emissions outside your direct control
Scope 2
Purchased electricity
Electricity your organization buys from a utility company or another supplier.
Purchased steam, heat and cooling
Steam, heat and cooling generated off-site used in buildings or processes your organization owns or controls.
.
Scope 3
Employee commuting
Modes of transportation except company owned vehicles (e.g., executive car, shuttle) used to get to and from work.
Commuting - Home office equipment
Equipment owned by your organization or employees to perform work activities remotely. This can include home heating, cooling and lighting.
Business Travel
Non-commuting travel via commercial planes, trains, taxis, rental cars and personal vehicles.
Purchased goods and services - Office equipment and supplies
Office supplies, furniture and IT equipment your organization purchases.
Purchased goods and services - Professional services
Professional and support services - for example, legal counsel, technical services or waste management.
Purchased goods and services - Online services
Outsourced telecom and information services - for example, data processing for your website.
Purchased goods and services - Other
Purchased goods or services not covered by other categories and excluding product or packaging related goods.
Waste generated in operations
Any waste your organization generates trash, recycling, compost etc.
Upstream leased assets - Facilities
Energy your organization uses but does not control in leased buildings (e.g., building owner controls lights and temperature).
